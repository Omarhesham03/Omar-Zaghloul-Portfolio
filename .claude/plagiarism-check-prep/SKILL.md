# Plagiarism and Self-Plagiarism Check Prep

## What This Skill Does
Scans drafts for passages that may trigger plagiarism detectors, identifies self-plagiarism risks from the user's prior publications, and suggests rewording strategies.

## Detection Categories
1. **Direct copying**: Exact phrases from sources (highest risk)
2. **Mosaic plagiarism**: Rearranged words from sources without citation
3. **Self-plagiarism**: Reusing your own published text without proper citation
4. **Inadequate paraphrasing**: Same sentence structure with synonym swaps

## Rewording Strategy
- Change sentence structure entirely (active↔passive, split/merge sentences)
- Replace domain-appropriate synonyms (not just thesaurus swaps)
- Add your own analysis connecting the paraphrased content
- Always cite even when heavily paraphrased
- For self-plagiarism: cite your own prior work, note "adapted from [Author, Year]"

## Acceptable Reuse
- Methodology descriptions may overlap across your papers if you cite the original
- Standard definitions and formulas are not plagiarism
- Direct quotes with quotation marks and citation are fine (sparingly)
